My North Star

A world where ideas are able to reach their telos.

Telos is a Greek concept meaning purposeful direction — the end a thing is moving toward. Not a fixed destiny, but a process of becoming.

• I believe ideas matter because they shape how the world works.

• I believe the difference between a good idea and a world-changing one is often execution and scale.

• I believe better tools — intellectual, financial, and technological — help ideas move closer to their intended impact.

• I’m drawn to understanding and working within those tools.

Bio

Who Am I

I’m a storyteller.

I’ve always been drawn to stories — how people explain the world, how ideas spread, and how meaning takes shape. I started college as a theatre major because I love taking something messy or hard to explain and turning it into a narrative that helps people see it differently.

I’m a systems thinker.

I like connecting dots and looking at how things work together — not just outcomes, but why they happen. I’m fascinated by how structures shape outcomes: markets, institutions, incentives, and human behavior. Finance and technology interest me because they make these structures visible, helping me see how ideas survive, fail, or scale in the real world.

I’m a problem solver.

I enjoy problems that don’t come with clear instructions. I like starting from uncertainty, asking better questions, and narrowing things down until something clicks.

I’m a risk navigator.

I love extreme sports — skiing, rock climbing, anything that forces full attention. To me, risk isn’t recklessness. It’s understanding what could go wrong, preparing for it, and deciding whether the reward is worth it.

I’m a world explorer, searching for my sukha.

Travel helps me feel grounded and open at the same time. I love seeing how people live, how they think, and how culture shapes what feels possible or impossible. Through moving between places and meeting different people, I’m slowly learning what sukha means to me.

I’m a service learner.
