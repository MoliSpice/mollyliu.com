Telos ‚Äî a Greek concept meaning purposeful direction, or the end a thing is moving toward.

Not a fixed destiny, but a process of becoming.

Who Am I

I'm a storyteller.

I've always been drawn to stories ‚Äî how people explain the world, how ideas spread, and how meaning takes shape.

I started college as a theatre major because I love taking something messy or hard to explain and turning it into a narrative that helps people see it differently. Theatre taught me that what moves people isn't just facts, but how those facts are structured and framed.

Stories help me make sense of complexity. When I write or speak, I'm usually trying to answer one simple question: what's really going on here?

I'm a systems thinker.

I like connecting dots and looking at how things work together ‚Äî not just outcomes, but why they happen.

I'm fascinated by how structures shape outcomes: markets, institutions, incentives, and human behavior. Finance and technology interest me because they make these structures visible, helping me see how ideas survive, fail, or scale in the real world.

I'm a problem solver.

I enjoy problems that don't come with clear instructions. I like starting from uncertainty, asking better questions, and slowly narrowing things down until something clicks.

I'm not attached to one discipline or method. I care more about understanding a problem well enough to choose the right tools ‚Äî and being willing to change my mind when the facts change.

I'm a risk navigator.

I love extreme sports ‚Äî skiing, rock climbing, anything that forces full attention.

To me, risk isn't recklessness. It's understanding what could go wrong, preparing for it, and deciding whether the reward is worth it. Before a climb or descent, I check my gear, assess my limits, and think through failure points.

That mindset carries into how I approach challenges more broadly: I don't avoid risk ‚Äî I try to understand it, manage it, and move forward with intention.

I'm a world explorer, searching for my sukha.

Travel helps me feel grounded and open at the same time. I love seeing how people live, how they think, and how culture shapes what feels possible or impossible.

Through moving between places and meeting different people, I'm slowly learning what sukha means to me ‚Äî a sense of ease, alignment, and inner clarity that comes from living honestly with the world, not escaping it.

I want a life where my perspective keeps expanding ‚Äî where I can see the breadth of the world, the depth of human nature, and the reality of society as it is ‚Äî while growing upward without losing the ability to empathize downward.

I'm a service learner.

I care deeply about education, especially for girls. Growing up in a traditional Eastern household where boys were often valued over girls shaped my belief that talent is everywhere, but access is not.

That belief led me to found Spotlaw, a theatre-based legal education project. After witnessing girls in rural China being exploited through scams and unlawful contracts, I realized education isn't just about knowledge ‚Äî it's about voice.

Spotlaw uses storytelling and performance to help students understand the law, question dominant narratives, and recognize their rights ‚Äî in ways rules and textbooks often cannot.
