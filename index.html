North Star

A world where ideas are able to reach their telos.

Telos is a Greek concept meaning purposeful direction — the end a thing is moving toward. Not a fixed destiny, but a process of becoming.

• I believe ideas matter because they shape how the world works.

• I believe the difference between a good idea and a world-changing one is often execution and scale.

• I believe better tools — intellectual, financial, and technological — help ideas move closer to their intended impact.

• I’m drawn to understanding and working within those tools.

Bio

Who Am I

This is the “everything” page — the parts of my story that don’t fit cleanly into an application box.

I’m a storyteller.

I’ve always been drawn to stories — how people explain the world, how ideas spread, and how meaning takes shape. I started college as a theatre major because I love taking something messy or hard to explain and turning it into a narrative that helps people see it differently. When I write or speak, I’m usually trying to answer one simple question: what’s really going on here?

I’m a systems thinker.

I like connecting dots and looking at how things work together — not just outcomes, but why they happen. I’m fascinated by how structures shape outcomes: markets, institutions, incentives, and human behavior. Finance and technology interest me because they make these structures visible, helping me see how ideas survive, fail, or scale in the real world.

I’m a problem solver.

I enjoy problems that don’t come with clear instructions. I like starting from uncertainty, asking better questions, and narrowing things down until something clicks. I’m not attached to one method — I care more about understanding the problem well enough to choose the right tools, and being willing to change my mind when the facts change.

I’m a risk navigator.

I love extreme sports — skiing, rock climbing, anything that forces full attention. To me, risk isn’t recklessness. It’s understanding what could go wrong, preparing for it, and deciding whether the reward is worth it. That mindset carries into how I approach challenges more broadly: I don’t avoid risk — I try to understand it, manage it, and move forward with intention.

I’m a world explorer, searching for my sukha.

Travel helps me feel grounded and open at the same time. I love seeing how people live, how they think, and how culture shapes what feels possible or impossible. Through moving between places and meeting different people, I’m slowly learning what sukha means to me — a sense of ease, alignment, and inner clarity that comes from living honestly with the world, not escaping it.

I’m a service learner.

I care deeply about education, especially for girls. Growing up in a traditional Eastern household where boys were often valued over girls shaped my belief that talent is everywhere, but access is not. That belief led me to found Spotlaw, a theatre-based legal education project that uses storytelling and performance to help students understand the law, question dominant narratives, and recognize their rights.

Spotlaw: spotlaw.org

I’m still becoming.

I don’t think of myself as finished or fixed. I’m more interested in direction than labels. What stays constant is my curiosity — about the world, about people, and about how ideas turn into reality.

Life Philosophy

A few beliefs I try to live by.

Make the world clearer. If I understand it deeply, I can explain it simply.
Chase truth, not vibes. I’d rather learn fast than be confidently wrong.
Risk is something to study. Fear isn’t a stop sign — it’s information.
Stay ambitious, stay human. Grow upward without losing empathy downward.
Let good ideas earn scale. Execution is where purpose becomes real.
Keep expanding perspective. Places and people change how I see.
